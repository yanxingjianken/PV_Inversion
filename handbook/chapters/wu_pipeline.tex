\chapter{The Wu Pipeline, Step by Step (Benchmark)}
\label{chap:wu_pipeline}

This chapter walks through the six executable steps in
\texttt{wu/steps/} that take the raw Wu Fortran output to the Davis 2022
Fig.~8 diagnostic.  Each step is illustrated with the figure it
generates (regenerated for this handbook into
\texttt{handbook/figs/wu/}).  This pipeline is the project
\textbf{benchmark}: the SH port of Part~II is judged against it.

\section{Step 05 --- Pass A/B: Mean and Event PV, Balanced \texorpdfstring{$\psi$}{psi}}
\label{sec:step05}

Step~05 compiles the three Fortran programs and runs \texttt{pvpialln}
twice: Pass~A on the 11-day mean state (\texttt{meanq}, \texttt{meanh})
and Pass~B on the 2025-01-08 event state (\texttt{event\_q.out},
\texttt{event\_h.out}).  The Ertel PV is computed on the interior
levels $925$--$250$\,hPa and the balanced streamfunction follows from
relative-vorticity inversion.

\section{Step 06 --- Pass C: Total Balanced Inversion (BALNC)}
\label{sec:step06}

Step~06 runs \texttt{qinvert21} (BALNC) to refine the event state into a
\emph{fully} balanced $(\psi,\Phi)$ pair (\texttt{event\_bal.out}), using
$\texttt{OMEGS}=\texttt{OMEGH}=1.4$, $\texttt{PART}=0.5$,
$\texttt{INLIN}=0$.

\begin{keybox}[Pass C confirms Pass B is already balanced]
For this case the Pass~C streamfunction is \emph{bit-identical} to the
Pass~B field: the refinement panel
(\Cref{fig:wu_passc_refine}, right) is exactly zero.  The total-balance
inversion therefore acts as a consistency check, confirming that the
\texttt{pvpialln} balanced state already satisfies the full BALNC
balance to solver tolerance.  (The solver reports hitting its iteration
cap while the increment stays at machine zero.)
\end{keybox}

\section{Step 07 --- Pass D: Piecewise Perturbation Inversion (BALP)}
\label{sec:step07}

Step~07 runs \texttt{qinvertp21} (BALP), which partitions $\dq$ into the
three vertical pieces of \Cref{sec:wu_passes} and inverts each to a
perturbation streamfunction $\psi'$ (\texttt{event\_pert.out}).  Piece~3
(the upper-tropospheric piece, $600$--$250$\,hPa) carries the dominant
blocking signal.

\section{Step 08 --- Parse Outputs and ERA5 Ertel PV}
\label{sec:step08}

Because Wu's internal $Q$ is in opaque solver units ($\sim\!600\times$
PVU), Step~08 computes the \emph{true} Ertel PV from the ERA5
$T,u,v$ fields in physical PVU,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{PV} = -g\,(\zeta+f)\,\frac{\partial\theta}{\partial p},
\label{eq:wu_ertel_pv}
\end{equation}
for use as PV-anomaly contours and as the PV field advected in Step~09.
The side-by-side comparison quantifies the $\approx\!600\times$ scaling
between Wu's $Q$ and physical PV.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/wu/wu_vs_era5_pv_250hpa.png}
  \caption[Wu Q vs ERA5 PV]{\textbf{Step 08.} $250$\,hPa Wu internal
  $Q$ (left) versus ERA5 Ertel PV in PVU (right).  The spatial patterns
  match; the amplitudes differ by the solver-internal scaling factor.}
  \label{fig:wu_vs_era5}
\end{figure}

\section{Step 09 --- PV Advection by the Induced Winds}
\label{sec:step09}

Step~09 forms the central Davis-Fig.~8 quantity: the $250$\,hPa
advection of total ERA5 PV by the piecewise-induced winds,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{PVadv} = -\Bigl(u'\,\frac{\partial q}{\partial x}
                       + v'\,\frac{\partial q}{\partial y}\Bigr)\times 86400
                 \quad [\text{PVU\,day}^{-1}],
\label{eq:wu_pvadv}
\end{equation}
where $(u',v')$ are the winds induced by each PV piece (Step~07/08) and
$q$ is the \emph{total} ERA5 PV (not Wu's $Q$).  The sign indicates
whether a piece's circulation advects PV cyclonically ($+$) or
anticyclonically ($-$) --- the mechanism by which the pieces sustain the
block.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/wu/pv_advection_all_pieces.png}
  \caption[PV advection per piece]{\textbf{Step 09.} $250$\,hPa PV
  advection by the winds induced by each of the three PV pieces.}
  \label{fig:wu_pvadv}
\end{figure}

\section{Step 10 --- Fig.~8 Replica (the Benchmark Result)}
\label{sec:step10}

Step~10 assembles the three-panel Davis 2022 Fig.~8 replica.  Each
panel (one per PV piece) overlays:
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{0pt}
  \item \textbf{filled colour:} $250$\,hPa PV advection
        (\cref{eq:wu_pvadv}), per-panel auto-scaled;
  \item \textbf{black contours:} total $250$\,hPa PV anomaly
        (solid positive, dashed negative);
  \item \textbf{arrows:} induced wind vectors (capped at $40$\,m\,s$^{-1}$).
\end{itemize}
Piece~3 receives $\sigma=1.5$ Gaussian smoothing and a wind cap to tame
the small-scale noise of \Cref{fig:wu_passd_noise}.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/wu/fig8_replica.png}
  \caption[Davis 2022 Fig.~8 replica]{\textbf{Step 10 --- the
  benchmark.} Three-panel replica of Davis et~al.\ (2022) Fig.~8 for the
  2025-01-08 California blocking event: PV advection (fill), PV anomaly
  (contours) and induced winds (arrows) for each PV piece.  This is the
  result the Python SH port (Part~II) aims to reproduce.}
  \label{fig:wu_fig8}
\end{figure}

\begin{keybox}[Benchmark status]
The Wu Fortran pipeline runs end-to-end and reproduces the Davis 2022
Fig.~8 structure.  It is the reference against which the SH BALNC port
of \Cref{chap:wu_balnc} is measured; at present the SH port reproduces
the inversion \emph{mathematics} but its total-balance pass does not yet
converge (\Cref{sec:wu_open}).
\end{keybox}
