\chapter{Introduction and Overview}
\label{chap:intro}

\section{Purpose of this Handbook}

This handbook documents \textbf{piecewise potential-vorticity inversion}
(PPVI) for atmospheric blocking, as implemented in
\texttt{pv\_inversion/}.  The goal of the project is to reproduce the
diagnostic of Davis et~al.\ (2022, \emph{J.\ Climate}, their Fig.~8) for
the 2025-01-08 California blocking event: partition the upper-level PV
anomaly into vertical pieces, invert each piece to a balanced
circulation, and quantify how each piece advects PV to sustain the
block.

The repository carries \textbf{two independent inversion tracks} that
share a common ERA5 data-preparation front end:

\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
  \item the \textbf{Wu Fortran reference} (\texttt{pv\_inversion/wu/}) ---
        W.~Wu's finite-difference successive-over-relaxation (SOR)
        solver (\texttt{pvpialln\_94UV.f}, \texttt{qinvert21\_94.f},
        \texttt{qinvertp21\_94.f}).  This track \textbf{works}: it
        reproduces the Davis 2022 Fig.~8 diagnostic end-to-end and is
        therefore treated as the \emph{benchmark}.  It is the subject of
        \textbf{Part~I} (\Cref{chap:wu_solver,chap:wu_pipeline}), where
        every pass is illustrated with the plot it generates.
  \item the \textbf{Python spherical-harmonic (SH) port}
        (\texttt{pv\_inversion/sh/}) --- a faithful re-implementation of
        Wu's non-dimensional balance system on the full-hemisphere SH
        grid, built on \texttt{pvtend.sh\_ops}.  This is the subject of
        \textbf{Part~II} (\Cref{chap:sh,chap:forward,chap:wu_balnc}).  It
        documents the mathematics, the operator mapping, and the
        \emph{current state} of the port, whose total-balance pass does
        not yet converge.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{keybox}[What was retired]
Two earlier SH solvers --- a one-step \emph{Direct Spectral Solve} and
an \emph{LGMRES + linearized-Charney} solver (collectively
``Family-B'') --- have been removed from the codebase and from this
handbook.  Their governing closure (the linear $f_0$-plane balance) is
retained in \Cref{sec:closure} because the SH BALNC port still cites it.
\end{keybox}

\section{Notation Cheat-Sheet}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
  \toprule
  Symbol & Meaning \\
  \midrule
  $\psi$, $\Phi$ & streamfunction (m$^2$\,s$^{-1}$), geopotential (m$^2$\,s$^{-2}$) \\
  $\bpsi$, $\bPhi$ & climatological mean (basic) state \\
  $\dpsi=\psi-\bpsi$, $\dPhi=\Phi-\bPhi$ & anomalies \\
  $q$ & Ertel potential vorticity (PVU = $10^{-6}\,$K\,m$^2$\,kg$^{-1}$\,s$^{-1}$) \\
  $\bq$, $\dq=q-\bq$ & mean and anomalous PV \\
  $\zeta=\Lap\psi$ & relative vorticity \\
  $f=2\Omega\sin\phi$, $f_0=f(45^\circ)$ & Coriolis parameter, reference value \\
  $\pi=(p/p_0)^{\kappa}$ & Exner function ($p_0=10^{5}$ Pa, $\kappa=R/c_p$) \\
  $\theta=T(p_0/p)^{\kappa}$ & potential temperature \\
  $\Aavo=f+\bzeta$, $\Bstb=\partial^2\bPhi/\partial\pi^2$ & avorticity, static stability proxy \\
  $\Lap$, $\Lapinv$ & horizontal Laplacian and its spectral inverse \\
  $D_1, D_2$ & first/second-derivative matrices in $\pi$ \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A more complete glossary appears in \Cref{app:notation}.
